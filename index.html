01 Executive Profile

AI researcher working at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence, Software Engineering, and Neurophysiology, focusing on how humans understand, trust, and interact with AI-generated systems. Holds a Ph.D. in Informatics Engineering (Intelligent Systems) from the University of Coimbra (summa cum laude).

Currently serves as Dean of the Faculty of Engineering & Information Technology and Director of the AI & Innovation Center at Palestine Ahliya University, and as an Integrated Researcher at CISUC. Leads an emerging research direction on neuroadaptive AI systems, introducing biofeedback-driven methods to model cognitive states such as comprehension, confusion, and trust during interaction with software and large language models.

Principal Investigator of the BRAIN project (2026), investigating physiological signals to evaluate and enhance human understanding of AI-generated code. His work has been published in leading venues including IEEE and Scientific Reports (Nature Portfolio), and has resulted in an international patent pending on AI-driven biofeedback systems for software evaluation.

His long-term research vision is to move beyond static AI systems toward cognitively-aware, adaptive systems that respond to human understanding in real time, enabling safer and more trustworthy human–AI collaboration.
